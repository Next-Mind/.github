## 1. Introduction

Mental health in educational environments has become a topic of high social and academic relevance, particularly after the impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic. Social isolation, abrupt changes in teaching models, and increased emotional pressures exposed weaknesses in the psychological support offered to students.

At the Technical School of Limeira (COTIL), there was no integrated technological structure to enable continuous monitoring of studentsâ€™ mental health, nor efficient communication between students, psychology professionals, and institutional administration.

NextMind was conceived as a comprehensive **multiplatform technological solution**, developed as part of an Integrative Project, aiming to fill this institutional gap.

---

## 2. Justification

The project is justified by the need to provide effective mental health support mechanisms in the school environment using accessible, organized, and secure digital tools.

The absence of specialized platforms hinders both the systematic monitoring of students and the integrated work of professionals, making the development of a centralized system essential.

---

## 3. Objectives

### 3.1 General Objective

Develop a **multiplatform computational system** aimed at monitoring and supporting studentsâ€™ mental health by integrating students, psychologists, and administrators into a secure and efficient digital environment.

### 3.2 Specific Objectives

- Develop a web application for psychologists;
- Implement a mobile application for students;
- Create a desktop application for system administrators;
- Develop a proprietary API for integration between platforms;
- Apply software engineering best practices;
- Promote accessibility, usability, and information security.

## 5. Methodology

The development followed a structured methodology combining field research, prototyping, incremental development, and academic validation.

### 5.1 Planning and Prototyping

- **Figma**: complete prototyping of all platforms  
- **Asana**: monitoring deadlines, tasks, and deliveries

### 5.2 Field Research

A survey was conducted at COTIL to understand:

- Student perception of mental health  
- Existing institutional support  
- Acceptance of a digital mental health platform

The results were highly positive, providing suggestions that were incorporated into the system.

### 5.3 Technologies Used

- **Mobile and Desktop**: Flutter, Firebase  
- **API**: PHP, Laravel  
- **Web**: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, TypeScript  
- **API Testing**: Postman (with own route documentation)

## 6. Results and Conclusion

The project was widely accepted within the academic environment, fully achieving its objectives.  
NextMind was recognized with **1st place at IV FECITEC** and received the **maximum score from the evaluation committee**, highlighting its technical and methodological quality and social relevance.
